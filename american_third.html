So quaint

by aquaintperson on 2/25/2013

What more can I say about a place with 1,800+ reviews and such beautiful ratings and photos? Picturesque little place on a downtown La Jolla corner, I've never had a bad experience in the multitude of brunches here over the last four years. Service is equivalent of their high food standards, my boyfriend's number one breakfast spot (mission coffee cup is a tie for me foodwise). The LJ eggs benedict keeps us coming back, with a perfect poach and smooth hollandaise every time. Love their potatoes on the side, as well as any drink or coffee we've tried (except the Mexican mocha, but willing to over look since asking for it cold made it seem like the issue of wateriness).

I love The Cottage!

by lovebite on 6/23/2012

One of my favorite breakfast spots in SD for sure! I'm an "omelette person", and the omelettes here are fantastic... near perfect even. Just the right amount of cheese and cooked to perfection! I try to avoid white flour, and pancakes aren't exactly healthy (yet I know some people embrace them as a food group). Shocking that something with the word "cake" in it isn't exactly a health food, I know... Occasionally I like to indulge in wheat pancakes though. Hey, they probably aren't that much better for you, but I'll buy into the illusion for sanity's sake. That being said, the wheat oatmeal pancakes here are OUT OF THIS WORLD. Add blueberries baked in and bananas on top = heavenly. I always order 2 on the side :D

Get these when you go!

by hatersgonnahate on 3/2/2013

My favorites are the sunday special (eggs benedict with crab) and the french toast stuffed with strawberries. Deliciously savory eggs benedict with a side of potatoes, and the french toast is great if you have a sweet tooth like me! The eggs la jolla is also very good but the crab cakes aren't my favorite for some reason. Don't mix up the sunday special eggs benedict with the regular crab cake benedict! They're quite different!
